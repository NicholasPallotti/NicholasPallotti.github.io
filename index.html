About

While I was born in Plano Texas, my parents soon decided to move to colorado to enjoy the phenomenal hiking skiing and camping the state has to offer (as well as escape the muggy heat of the lone star state). Despite my parents' enthusiasm for the great outdoors I always gravitated towards the indoors, enjoying video games, card games like Magic The Gathering and board games. Being born in the nineties meant I had the experience of growing up with much of the technology that we use on a daily basis today. Watching i-pods and Razor flip phones develop into i-phones, the internet develop into what it is today, and The overall breakneck speed at which technology has evolved drove a fascination with that technology in me, and led to my decision to pursue computer science.

I studied Computer science at Colorado State University, and during that time I learned many of the valuable skills required for a developer, but some of the most valuable skills I learned can't be quantified by how long I've been coding in a language or using an IDE. the need to program in a variety of languages, using a wider variety of IDE's, tools and frameworks has given me the ability to not just excel at technologies I already know, but also quickly learn new ones. In addition to developing the ability to learn and adapt quickly I learned valuable skills such as working in a group in an Agile development environment, CI/CD workflows, test driven development, and the skills needed to communicate technical concepts to both end users and peers.
